Resources to develop Disaster Awareness

Earthquake

An earthquake is a sudden ground shaking caused by shifting tectonic plates. Awareness includes knowing safety measures, such as "Drop, Cover, and Hold On," and having an emergency plan in place to reduce risks and ensure safety during and after a quake.

Floods

A flood is the overflow of water onto normally dry land, often caused by heavy rain or melting snow. Awareness includes monitoring weather alerts, knowing evacuation routes, and avoiding driving or walking through floodwaters.

Watch Video

Landslide

A landslide is the sudden movement of rock, soil, and debris down a slope due to gravity. Awareness involves recognizing warning signs like cracks in the ground, having an evacuation plan, and avoiding areas prone to landslides during heavy rain or after earthquakes.

Watch Video

Tsunami

A tsunami is a series of large ocean waves triggered by underwater earthquakes or volcanic eruptions. Awareness involves recognizing tsunami warnings, moving to higher ground immediately, and avoiding the shore during such alerts.

Watch Video

Cyclone

A cyclone is a powerful tropical storm with strong winds and heavy rain. Awareness involves monitoring weather forecasts, preparing an emergency kit, and having an evacuation plan ready.

Watch Video

Thunder and Lightning

Thunder and lightning are electrical discharges during storms. Awareness involves staying indoors, avoiding tall objects and water, and waiting 30 minutes after the last lightning before going outside.

Watch Video

Drought

A drought is an extended period of below-average rainfall, leading to water shortages. Awareness includes conserving water, monitoring local conditions, and following water use restrictions.

Watch Video

Heat Waves

A heat wave is an extended period of excessively high temperatures. Awareness involves staying hydrated, avoiding strenuous activities during peak heat, and using cooling methods like fans and air conditioning.

Watch Video

Cold Wave

A cold wave is an extended period of unusually low temperatures. Awareness involves dressing warmly, avoiding prolonged exposure to the cold, and ensuring proper heating in your home.

Watch Video

Forest Fire

Forest fires are uncontrolled blazes in wooded areas, often caused by human activity or natural events. Awareness involves following fire bans, creating defensible space around properties, and having an evacuation plan ready.

Watch Video

Radiological and Nuclear Emergency

A nuclear emergency involves accidental release of radioactive materials. Awareness includes knowing evacuation routes, sheltering indoors, and listening to official instructions for safety and protective measures.

Watch Video

Domestic Fires

Domestic fires are fires that occur within homes. Awareness includes installing smoke alarms, having an escape plan, and keeping fire extinguishers handy.

Watch Video

Basic Life Support

Basic Life Support (BLS) involves emergency techniques like CPR and using an AED to help someone with cardiac arrest or breathing issues. Awareness includes learning BLS skills through training courses and knowing how to apply them in emergencies.

Watch Video

Basic Search and Rescue

Basic search and rescue involves locating and helping people in distress, often after disasters. Awareness includes knowing how to use essential tools, following safety protocols, and receiving proper training for effective rescue operations.
